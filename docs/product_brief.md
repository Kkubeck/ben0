# BEN-0 Product Brief

## What it is

BEN-0 (Botanical Engram Node) is a local-first stewardship tool for botanical garden living collections. It ingests accession and item data, flags likely record problems, indexes internal documents, and provides a cautious assistant that can help staff inspect evidence without taking over curator authority.

## Who it is for

- Curators of living collections
- Registrars and collections managers
- Propagation nursery staff
- Conservation horticulture teams
- Garden IT / data support staff
- Small institutions that need useful tooling without enterprise overhead

## Why it matters

Botanical collections often sit on decades of uneven data entry, changing accession systems, undocumented provenance assumptions, and scattered institutional memory. BEN-0 is meant to reduce that fragility.

It helps institutions:

- make collection records easier to audit
- surface missing provenance and status inconsistencies
- preserve policy documents and accession notes in a searchable local index
- route likely corrections into reviewable tickets
- keep sensitive data under institutional control

## Positioning

BEN-0 is not a replacement for IrisBG, BG-BASE, or a full collections management system. It is a stewardship layer: a local companion that helps staff understand, clean, and defend the quality of the data they already have.

## Design stance

- local-first by default
- deterministic wherever possible
- model-assisted only where helpful
- human review required for consequential changes
- suitable for real institutional data on an ordinary laptop
